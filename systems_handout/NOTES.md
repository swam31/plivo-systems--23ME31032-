My design implements Forward Error Correction by having each transmitted packet carry the mathematical XOR of the two previous payloads (FEC(N) = Payload(N-1) XOR Payload(N-2)). This provides instant chain-reaction recovery for up to two consecutive packet losses without the severe round-trip latency penalties of ARQ retransmissions. To strictly respect the 2.0x overhead cap, a Token Bucket dynamically throttles redundancy, and the sequence number is compressed to a single byte over the wire to maximize bandwidth efficiency. The playout delay_ms you should grade at is 102ms. Because the redundancy spans a 40ms time-diversity window, the solution is highly resilient to isolated drops and minor bursts. What breaks this design is a severe network blackout burst lasting longer than 40ms, which would destroy the redundancy chain and cause unrecoverable glitches. It can also be broken by excessive OS thread scheduling jitter pushing the 100ms packet arrivals past the razor-thin 102ms deadline.
