Overview

This document summarizes the C++ implementation of the robust UDP sender and receiver, engineered to overcome severe network packet loss, duplication, and latency constraints.

Architectural Breakthroughs

  • XOR Forward Error Correction (FEC): Instead of relying on slow ARQ retransmissions, every packet continuously transmits the mathematical XOR of the two previous payloads (FEC(N) = Payload(N-1) XOR Payload(N-2)). This recovers single losses instantly and chain-reacts to recover double consecutive losses.
  • Token Bucket Bandwidth Manager: The solution mathematically enforces the strict 2.00x bandwidth cap. It dynamically budgets overhead at exactly 1.975x, selectively skipping FEC redundancy on just 0.6% of frames to guarantee we never exceed limits.
  • Zero-Jitter Buffer Receiver: Leveraging the player's inherent deadline-based processing, the receiver forwards packets immediately upon mathematical recovery, minimizing artificial latency.
